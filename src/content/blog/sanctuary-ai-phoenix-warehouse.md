## Beyond Single-Task Robots

Sanctuary AI has announced that its Phoenix humanoid robot can now autonomously perform over 50 distinct warehouse tasks — a significant leap from the dozen or so tasks it could handle just six months ago. The improvement comes from advances in the company's Carbon AI system, which serves as the robot's general-purpose intelligence layer.

The tasks range from simple pick-and-place operations to more complex activities like quality inspection, inventory counting, package labeling, and workstation cleanup.

## How Carbon Works

Carbon is Sanctuary's proprietary AI system that combines several approaches:

- **Large behavior models** trained on thousands of hours of human teleoperation data
- **Real-time visual reasoning** that lets the robot understand and adapt to changing environments
- **Task decomposition** that breaks complex instructions into executable subtasks
- **Continuous learning** that improves performance through ongoing operation

What makes Carbon notable is its approach to generalization. Rather than training specialized models for each task, Sanctuary trains a single large model that develops transferable manipulation and reasoning skills.

## The Teleoperation Pipeline

A key part of Sanctuary's strategy is its teleoperation system. Human operators can remotely control Phoenix robots, and every minute of that operation becomes training data for Carbon. This creates a flywheel effect — more deployed robots means more data, which means better autonomous capability, which means more commercial value, which funds more deployments.

The company reports that tasks which initially required 100% teleoperation now run at approximately 80% autonomy, with human oversight needed only for edge cases and exceptions.

## Commercial Deployment

Sanctuary has active pilot programs with several major logistics and retail companies. The company's business model focuses on robotics-as-a-service, charging customers an hourly rate rather than selling robots outright.

This approach removes the capital expenditure barrier for customers and ensures Sanctuary maintains its robots and continuously improves their capabilities through over-the-air updates.

## The General-Purpose Vision

Sanctuary's ultimate goal is building what it calls "general-purpose robots" — machines that can perform any physical task a human can. The 50-task milestone, while impressive, is still a fraction of the hundreds of distinct tasks a typical warehouse worker performs. But the trajectory is encouraging, and each new task added to Phoenix's repertoire becomes easier than the last thanks to the transfer learning capabilities of Carbon.
