## Record-Breaking Round

Figure AI has closed a $2.6 billion Series B funding round, valuing the Sunnyvale-based startup at $18 billion — making it one of the most valuable robotics companies in the world. The round was led by Bezos Expeditions and Microsoft, with strategic participation from BMW Group, Amazon Industrial Innovation Fund, and Intel Capital.

The funding will be used to build Figure's first dedicated manufacturing facility, hire an additional 500 engineers, and accelerate the rollout of Figure 02 to commercial customers.

## Figure 02: Built for Work

Unlike consumer-focused humanoid concepts, Figure 02 is designed explicitly for industrial environments. The robot stands 5'6", weighs 130 lbs, and features:

- **Full-body dexterity** with 16 degrees of freedom per arm
- **4-hour continuous operation** on a single battery charge
- **Natural language task instruction** powered by a custom foundation model developed in partnership with OpenAI
- **Onboard compute** capable of real-time scene understanding and task planning

## The BMW Connection

BMW has been the most visible early adopter of Figure's technology. The automaker began piloting Figure 01 units in its Spartanburg, South Carolina plant in late 2025, focusing on body shop and logistics tasks.

According to BMW's manufacturing VP, the Figure robots have shown promising results in tote carrying, part inspection, and workstation tending — tasks that are ergonomically challenging for human workers over long shifts.

## The Path to Mass Production

Figure's CEO has outlined an ambitious timeline:

1. **Q3 2026**: Complete construction of manufacturing facility in Sunnyvale
2. **Q4 2026**: Begin pilot production run of 100 units
3. **Q2 2027**: Ramp to 1,000 units/month capacity
4. **2028**: Target unit cost below $50,000

The company acknowledges that mass-producing humanoid robots presents unique challenges in quality control, supply chain management, and after-sales support that the industry hasn't fully solved.

## Competitive Landscape

Figure isn't alone in this space. Tesla, Unitree, Agility Robotics, Apptronik, and 1X Technologies are all pursuing commercial humanoid deployments. But Figure's combination of well-funded R&D, strategic automotive partnerships, and a clear commercial focus gives it a strong position in what's becoming an increasingly competitive market.
